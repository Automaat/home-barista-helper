## ‚òï ESPRESSO (Timemore 078S)

### üéöÔ∏è Grinder Settings

| Roast Level | Timemore 078S Setting |
| ----------- | --------------------- |
| Light       | 0.6 - 1.4             |
| Medium      | 1.5 - 2.0             |
| Dark        | 2.0 - 2.6             |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "For espresso, the range seems to be fairly narrow, between 0.6 to 1.4 on some units" [Source: Honest Coffee Guide]

> üìÑ **STATED**: "The grinder operates across a range of 235 ‚Äì 1235 microns" [Source: Honest Coffee Guide]

‚ö†Ô∏è **CALIBRATION NOTE**: "Many 078S units were shipped with the pin 1 to 2 holes too coarse" - calibrate zero point first!

### üìê Brew Ratios by Roast

| Roast Level | Brew Ratio  | Example (18g dose) |
| ----------- | ----------- | ------------------ |
| Dark        | 1:1.5       | 18g ‚Üí 27g          |
| Medium      | 1:2         | 18g ‚Üí 36g          |
| Light       | 1:2.5 - 1:4 | 18g ‚Üí 45-72g       |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "A 1:2 ratio is recommended by professional coffee institutes as the starting point" [Source: Espresso AF]

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Dark roasts are much easier to extract and allow for a lower brew ratio. For a dark roast, you can go for a 1:1.5 ratio" [Source: Various]

### ‚è±Ô∏è Shot Timing

- **Target Range**: 25-35 seconds from first drip
- **Light Roasts Exception**: Can exceed 40+ seconds

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Don't worry too much about going over the recommended 25-35 seconds... It's not uncommon to pull a delicious light roast that takes >40 seconds to reach target yield" [Source: Espresso AF]

### üå°Ô∏è Temperature

- **Default**: 93.5¬∞C
- **Light roasts**: Higher (up to 96¬∞C)
- **Dark roasts**: Lower (90-92¬∞C)

### üîß Dialing-In Protocol

```
1. LOCK DOSE ‚Üí Choose based on basket (e.g., 18g)
2. SET RATIO ‚Üí Start at 1:2 (18g in ‚Üí 36g out)
3. ADJUST GRIND ‚Üí Target 25-35s extraction
   - Too fast (<20s) ‚Üí grind FINER
   - Too slow (>40s) ‚Üí grind COARSER
4. TASTE & ADJUST RATIO:
   - Sour ‚Üí INCREASE ratio (try 18g/40g, then 18g/44g)
   - Bitter ‚Üí DECREASE ratio (try 18g/32g)
   - Bitter AND Sour ‚Üí Uneven extraction, fix puck prep
5. REFINE GRIND ‚Üí As fine as possible without channeling
```

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Grind as fine as possible while maintaining balance... 'Too fine' causes channeling and faster flow" [Source: Espresso AF]

## ü´ñ HARIO V60 (Commandante C40)

### üéöÔ∏è Grinder Settings

| Cup Size       | Standard Axle | Red Clix     |
| -------------- | ------------- | ------------ |
| Single (~350g) | 23-28 clicks  | 46-52 clicks |
| Double (~700g) | 28-32 clicks  | 52-57 clicks |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "V60: 23-32 clicks (Standard-axle), 46-57 clicks (Red Clix)" [Source: Coffeedesk]

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Comandante C40 uses approximately 30 microns per click" [Source: Basic Barista]

### üìê Recipe Parameters

| Parameter       | Target                   |
| --------------- | ------------------------ |
| Ratio           | 1:15 - 1:16              |
| Temperature     | 90-96¬∞C                  |
| Bloom           | 2x coffee weight, 30-45s |
| Total Brew Time | 2:45 - 3:15 (inc. bloom) |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Use a coffee-to-water ratio of 1:15 as your starting point, which means 20g of coffee to 300ml of water" [Source: Low Key Coffee Snobs]

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Brew time target: 2:45-3:15 minutes' time (the 30-second bloom included)" [Source: Coffeedesk]

### üî• V60: Roast-Specific Parameters

| Parameter        | Light Roast    | Medium Roast | Dark Roast   |
| ---------------- | -------------- | ------------ | ------------ |
| Grind (Standard) | 22-25 clicks   | 25-28 clicks | 28-32 clicks |
| Grind (Red Clix) | 44-50 clicks   | 50-56 clicks | 56-64 clicks |
| Temperature      | 96¬∞C (boiling) | 93¬∞C         | 90¬∞C         |
| Ratio            | 1:16 - 1:17    | 1:15 - 1:16  | 1:14 - 1:15  |
| Brew Time        | 3:00 - 3:30    | 2:45 - 3:15  | 2:30 - 3:00  |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "For light roasted coffee, many tend to use boiling water" [Source: Niche Coffee]

### üîß Dialing-In Protocol

```
1. SET RATIO ‚Üí 1:15 (20g coffee : 300g water)
2. START GRIND ‚Üí Based on roast (see table above)
3. BLOOM ‚Üí 40-50g water, wait 30-45s
4. POUR ‚Üí Slow circles, center to edge, 300g total by 2:00
5. DRAIN ‚Üí Should finish by 3:00-3:15
6. TASTE & ADJUST:
   - Sour/weak ‚Üí FINER grind OR hotter water
   - Bitter/harsh ‚Üí COARSER grind OR cooler water
   - Too fast (<2:30) ‚Üí grind FINER
   - Too slow (>3:30) ‚Üí grind COARSER
```

> üìÑ **STATED**: "A grind that's too fine will stall the drawdown and make your coffee bitter; too coarse and it will rush through and taste weak" [Source: Daniel Norris]

### üîß Dialing-In Protocol (Standard Recipe)


## üéØ Universal Troubleshooting Matrix

| Taste                | Problem              | Solution                                                |
| -------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sour**             | Under-extracted      | Grind finer, higher temp, longer time, increase ratio   |
| **Bitter**           | Over-extracted       | Grind coarser, lower temp, shorter time, decrease ratio |
| **Sour + Bitter**    | Uneven extraction    | Fix puck prep (espresso), pour technique (filter)       |
| **Weak/watery**      | Low extraction       | Finer grind, more coffee, higher temp                   |
| **Harsh/astringent** | Fines over-extracted | Coarser grind, better grinder, lower temp               |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "If you change your grind size, water temperature, and coffee ratio all at once, you won't know which factor did what" [Source: HexNub]

## üî• Roast Level Adjustment Guide

### Why Roast Level Matters

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Light roasts are denser and less porous, requiring finer grinding to slow down
> extraction and draw out delicate citrus, floral, and tea-like notes" [Source: QIKA Coffee]

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Dark roasts are more brittle and porous, needing a slightly coarser grind to
> prevent over-extraction and bitterness" [Source: QIKA Coffee]

### The Science

| Roast Level | Bean Density | Solubility | Extraction Speed |
| ----------- | ------------ | ---------- | ---------------- |
| Light       | High (dense) | Low        | Slow             |
| Medium      | Medium       | Medium     | Medium           |
| Dark        | Low (porous) | High       | Fast             |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "The fundamental principle: Finer grounds create more surface area for water to
> interact with, increasing extraction efficiency" [Source: Savor Suite]

### Grind Adjustment by Roast

**When switching roasts, adjust 2-3 clicks:**

| Switching From ‚Üí To | Grind Adjustment                 |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Dark ‚Üí Light        | Go FINER (2-3 clicks)            |
| Light ‚Üí Dark        | Go COARSER (2-3 clicks)          |
| Medium ‚Üí Light      | Go slightly FINER (1-2 clicks)   |
| Medium ‚Üí Dark       | Go slightly COARSER (1-2 clicks) |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "The difference between optimal and suboptimal grinds can be just 2-3 clicks on
> a quality grinder" [Source: Savor Suite]

### Temperature Adjustment by Roast

| Roast Level | Temperature | Reasoning                               |
| ----------- | ----------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Light       | 94-96¬∞C     | Higher temp accelerates slow extraction |
| Medium      | 92-94¬∞C     | Balanced                                |
| Dark        | 88-92¬∞C     | Lower temp prevents over-extraction     |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "The normal rule is lower temperatures for darker roasts and higher temperatures
> for lighter roasts" [Source: Perfect Daily Grind]

### Espresso: Roast-Specific Parameters

| Parameter       | Light Roast     | Dark Roast       |
| --------------- | --------------- | ---------------- |
| Grind texture   | Fine table salt | Granulated sugar |
| Extraction time | 28-35s          | 22-28s           |
| Brew ratio      | 1:2 to 1:2.5    | 1:1.5 to 1:2     |
| Temperature     | 94-96¬∞C         | 88-92¬∞C          |

> üìÑ **STATED**: "Light roast beans maintain higher density and harder structure, making them
> naturally resistant to water penetration" [Source: Savor Suite]

### Your Grinders: Roast Adjustment

### Brew Time Adjustment

> üìÑ **STATED**: "The lighter the roast, the longer the steep, as this allows more flavours to
> develop" [Source: Spiller & Tait]

| Roast Level | Time Adjustment |
| ----------- | --------------- |
| Light       | +15-30s longer  |
| Medium      | Baseline        |
| Dark        | -15-30s shorter |

### Quick Decision Matrix

```
IF switching to LIGHTER roast:
   ‚Üí Grind FINER (2-3 clicks)
   ‚Üí Temperature HIGHER (+2-4¬∞C)
   ‚Üí Time LONGER (+15-30s)
   ‚Üí Ratio can be HIGHER (more water)

IF switching to DARKER roast:
   ‚Üí Grind COARSER (2-3 clicks)
   ‚Üí Temperature LOWER (-2-4¬∞C)
   ‚Üí Time SHORTER (-15-30s)
   ‚Üí Ratio can be LOWER (less water)
```
